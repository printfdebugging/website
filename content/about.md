I am Sahil Gautam, from India. I live  in the state Haryana, which is in the north
of  New  Delhi.  During  the  second  year of  my  college  I  got  involved  with
[libreoffice development], and one  year later worked on a GSoC  project. Now I am
working for an open source company [allotropia].

I have been daily-driving  linux for the past two years, and  I strongly feel that
we need  better applications  for linux.  Thunderbird sucks,  most of  the desktop
environments suck as they  seem to leave behind one group of  users for the other,
and are  bloated in general.  Code editors  suck because they  require significant
investment of time, and even then are good at  one job. Vim is fast, but is a TUI.
Emacs  supports GUI  but is  barely usable  with large  files. VsCode  is electron
based, and when it lags, it sucks the life out of you.

Programming languages are also in a quite  wierd shape. Some people praise OOP and
use C++,  while others dispise  it saying "it leaves  a decade worth  of computing
performance on the table for neat organization". **I like C**.
